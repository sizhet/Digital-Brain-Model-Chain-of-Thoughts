# Digital Brain Model — Chain-of-Thoughts Documents (DBM-COT)
# General Preface

The Digital Brain Model (DBM) is a system-engineering framework composed of structural intelligence, metric-space reasoning, differential trees, the Event Language Model (ELM), the structural language CCGL, autonomous evolution mechanisms (Mutations Engine), and multi-agent alignment structures (Fusion Cortex).
It is not an extension of existing LLMs, but a new paradigm of incremental intelligence—a system capable of building its own structures, accumulating knowledge, and evolving along task chains and mutation chains.

The DBM-COT (Chain-of-Thoughts Documents) corpus records the full developmental trajectory of DBM—from foundational insights and theoretical derivations to algorithmic design, Java implementations, and reproducible engineering prototypes.
It serves as DBM’s Structured Thought Ledger, a traceable and evolvable infrastructure for future research and engineering.

DBM-COT aims to:

Present DBM’s conceptual evolution through explicit, structured thought chains.

Provide reproducible algorithms, interfaces, and testing frameworks for future open-source development.

Establish a stable and extensible foundation for students, educators, researchers, and industry partners.

The following three sections provide tailored guidance for different categories of readers.

Learning DBM is not exploring something foreign;
it is rediscovering abilities you already possess, now made explicit, computable, and engineerable.
